# Business Process Alignment & Redesign — Reference Guide

**CV Competency:** Business Process Alignment & Redesign
**Scope:** Cross-cutting — stages 1, 2, 3
**Tracked In:** CONTRIBUTION-LOG.md (per programme)

---

## What This Competency Covers

The ability to analyse current-state business processes, identify misalignments with Temenos Transact capabilities, and redesign processes to leverage packaged functionality while minimising customisation.

## Key Skills

- Process mapping and documentation (current-state vs target-state)
- Identifying process-to-capability alignment gaps
- Facilitating process redesign workshops with business SMEs
- Evaluating "adopt the standard" vs "customise to fit" trade-offs
- Documenting redesigned processes with clear acceptance criteria
- Managing process change impact on people, training, and operations

## When to Apply

| Stage | Activity |
|---|---|
| 1 — Discovery | Map current-state processes, identify pain points |
| 2 — Fit-Gap & Process Design | Assess process alignment with Transact, redesign gaps |
| 3 — Solution Architecture | Validate redesigned processes against target architecture |

## Tracking

This competency is cross-cutting — it does not own a specific artefact folder. Contributions are logged in each programme's `CONTRIBUTION-LOG.md` and may reference artefacts in `03-fit-gap-register/` and `02-target-state-reference-architecture/`.

## Quality Signals

- Current-state processes are documented, not assumed
- Redesigned processes have clear before/after comparisons
- Process changes have impact assessments (people, training, ops)
- Business SMEs have validated redesigned processes
- Process redesign decisions are linked to fit-gap scores

## Steering Committee Value

Process alignment reduces customisation cost by maximising adoption of Transact standard functionality. Redesigned processes with clear acceptance criteria de-risk UAT and accelerate user adoption.

## CV Narrative

> "I led process alignment workshops across X business domains, redesigning Y processes to adopt Transact standard functionality. This prevented Z customisations, saving £Xm and reducing implementation risk."
